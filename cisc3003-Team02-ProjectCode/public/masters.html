Roadmap

An 18-month application timeline

  1. 1

    Year 2 · Explore

    Find the intersection of "interesting" and "good at"

    • Take one research-heavy elective you'd enjoy reading about on a Saturday.
    • Read three abstracts from conferences in that subfield (NeurIPS, SIGCOMM, CHI — depending on area).
    • Approach a professor about a small research task; even volunteer work counts.
    • Academic
    • Low stakes
  2. 2

    Year 3 Fall · Shortlist

    Draft a programme list by region and tier

    • Pick 3 reach, 4 target and 3 safety programmes (total 10).
    • For each, note: faculty whose work you'd want to join, deadlines, required tests, tuition.
    • Start following those labs on X, GitHub or group pages.
    • Logistics
  3. 3

    Year 3 Spring · Research + tests

    Produce one artefact and book one exam

    • Aim for a small research contribution (code, dataset, or poster) you can point to.
    • Schedule TOEFL/IELTS first, GRE only if required by target programmes.
    • Start a spreadsheet of every programme's deadline and required document.
    • Academic
    • Logistics
  4. 4

    Summer before Year 4 · Essays + letters

    Draft your Statement of Purpose and ask for letters

    • Write a SoP that connects what you've done → why this programme → what you'll do next.
    • Ask recommenders 8+ weeks before the first deadline. Give them a bullet list of context.
    • Polish CV to fit the genre expected by graduate committees (not industry resumes).
    • Writing
    • High leverage
  5. 5

    Year 4 Fall · Applications

    Submit on a staggered schedule

    • Submit earliest deadlines in early October; most December deadlines by mid-December.
    • Keep a cold-submission log: date, confirmation number, documents uploaded.
    • Don't edit your SoP after submission. Move on.
    • Execution
  6. 6

    Year 4 Spring · Decisions

    Compare offers on fit, not prestige alone

    • Talk to current students at each admitted programme before deciding.
    • Ask about PhD placement, funding continuity and supervisor availability.
    • Decline other offers promptly — someone on the waitlist thanks you.
    • Decision

Deep dives

Essays, letters, tests and research

Statement of Purpose

A strong SoP is specific. It names the exact faculty, projects and methods that draw you to a programme. Generic SoPs are invisible. Aim for 3 paragraphs on past experience, 1 on present motivation, 2 on future work and fit, 1 on a concrete "why this programme". Keep it under 1000 words.

Recommendation letters

Ask people who have seen you produce work, not just take exams. One strong letter from your research supervisor beats three polite letters from lecturers. Give recommenders: your CV, your SoP draft, the programme list with deadlines, and 3 bullet points on what you hope the letter highlights.

CV for graduate applications

Graduate CVs look different from industry resumes. Lead with education, then research experience, then publications or projects, then teaching, then industry. Expected length: 2 pages, never 1. Include a skills section but don't rely on it.

Standardized tests

TOEFL/IELTS is required almost universally for non-native-English applicants. GRE is less commonly required post-2020; verify per programme. Target TOEFL 100+ / IELTS 7.0+ for top-tier programmes. Take the test once, study for six weeks, and move on.

Research experience

Most admissions committees want evidence that you've survived ambiguity. A published paper is the gold standard; a finished capstone project with a clear technical contribution is also strong. Volunteer work in a lab for one semester counts if you can articulate what you did.

UM-specific tips

Start visiting faculty office hours in Year 2 — research pairings at UM often come from sustained conversations, not one-off emails. The FST research groups in AI, cybersecurity and data science are active and open to motivated undergraduates.

UM's exchange agreements with overseas universities can put you into a credit-bearing semester at a target school; check SAO well in advance.
