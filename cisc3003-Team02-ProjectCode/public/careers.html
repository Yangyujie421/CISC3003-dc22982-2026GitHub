Chapter 04

Career paths

Nine possible directions after a CS undergrad — what the daily work looks like, what skills matter, and where to go next.

Software engineering

The default path. Builds and maintains the systems that everyone else uses. Roles span web, mobile, backend infra, DevOps, and platform.

  • DSA
  • Systems
  • Git

Typical roles: SWE, Full-stack, Backend, SRE, Platform.

See SDE openings →

Machine learning engineering

Builds production ML systems — training pipelines, inference infrastructure, model evaluation. Requires strong ML fundamentals and software engineering chops.

  • PyTorch
  • DSA
  • Statistics

Typical roles: MLE, Applied Scientist, ML Platform Engineer.

See MLE openings →

Data science & analytics

Turns product and business questions into measurable experiments. SQL-heavy with a healthy dose of causal inference and experimental design.

  • SQL
  • Statistics
  • Python

Typical roles: Data Scientist, Analytics Engineer, Product DS.

See Data openings →

Security engineering

Protects products, networks and data. Specialisations include application security (AppSec), red-teaming, detection, and cryptography.

  • Networks
  • Systems
  • Cryptography

Typical roles: Security Engineer, Pen Tester, Detection Engineer.

See Security openings →

Research (industry or academia)

Pushes the field forward with papers and prototypes. Usually requires a PhD in industry labs (DeepMind, FAIR, MSR); some ML companies hire Bachelor's-level research engineers.

  • Math
  • Writing
  • Literature review

Typical roles: Research Scientist, Research Engineer, PhD student.

See Research openings →

Product management (tech PM)

Decides what engineering should build and why. CS background helps enormously in technical PM roles at Google, Meta, Microsoft.

  • Communication
  • Analytics
  • UX sense

Typical roles: APM, RPM, Technical PM, Growth PM.

See PM openings →

DevOps & infrastructure

Owns the reliability and deployability of other teams' work. Cloud-heavy, automation-heavy, and — at scale — extremely interesting systems work.

  • Linux
  • Cloud
  • CI/CD

Typical roles: DevOps, SRE, Platform, Cloud Engineer.

Mobile engineering

iOS (Swift) or Android (Kotlin). Specialises at a platform-level and carries deep expertise other SDE generalists don't have.

  • Swift / Kotlin
  • UX
  • Performance

Typical roles: iOS Engineer, Android Engineer.

Game development

Often overlooked by CS grads but rewarding. Mix of graphics, engine programming, and very cross-functional work with artists and designers.

  • C++
  • Graphics
  • Linear algebra

Typical roles: Gameplay Engineer, Engine Programmer, Technical Artist.

How to pick

Three honest framings

By interest

Watch what you do on a Sunday afternoon when no one's grading you. That's a signal. Don't dismiss it.

By lifestyle

Product-facing roles trade variety for ambiguity. Infra trades polish for depth. Research trades pace for patience. All three can be good lives.

By geography

SDE is the most globally portable. MLE and research cluster in a handful of cities. Security is growing everywhere. PM is the least portable.

Don't pick forever, pick for next

Your first role does not define your career. Most senior engineers have pivoted at least once. Pick the first job where you'll learn fastest from people more senior than you — that matters more than title, team or even salary early on.
