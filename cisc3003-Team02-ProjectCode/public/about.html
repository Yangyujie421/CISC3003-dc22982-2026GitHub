Chapter 01

About the purpose of this site

A short, honest explanation of what this handbook is, who it's for, and how to get the most out of it.

Why we built this

Most undergraduate CS students already know they should study hard, build projects, and think about graduate school or full-time offers. The problem isn't motivation — it's the absence of a single place to compare skills, programmes and job paths without sifting through twenty different forum threads.

Cornerstone is our attempt at that single place. It's written by CS students at the University of Macau, for CS students anywhere in the world who want concrete, specific information they can actually act on — not a pep talk.

What you'll find inside

  • Skills Development — a concrete list of what to build, when to build it.
  • Master's Preparation — a realistic timeline and 37 programmes across four regions.
  • Career Paths — six tracks with the skills and next-steps for each.
  • Jobs — 43 real openings from top companies, filterable by role.
  • Job Readiness — a four-stage workflow from resume to offer.
  • Reflection — the story of building it.

At a glance

Audience: Undergraduate CS students (Year 1–4).

Scope: Technical skills, graduate school, and early-career job paths.

Source material: Official university and company pages, with summaries distilled to one sentence per item.

Updated: 2026

Reading time: ~25 minutes end-to-end.

Three pillars

Orient · Prepare · Reflect

Orient

Before you can prepare, you need a map. The Skills and Careers chapters give you the lay of the land — what exists, what matters, and what actually gets built in industry.

Prepare

The Master's, Jobs and Readiness chapters are the "how". Timelines, programmes, openings and step-by-step checklists for moving from "interested" to "applying".

Reflect

Action without reflection is noise. The Reflection chapter closes the loop — what changed along the way, what we would do differently, and how we think about the next few years.

Common questions

FAQ

Who wrote this?

Two undergraduate CS students at the University of Macau, as our CISC3003 Web Programming group project. We wrote the content based on our own reading and research; we're not career counsellors.

Is this advice UM-specific?

Some of it is (e.g., the UM-specific tips callout on Master's Preparation). Most of the skills, timeline, career tracks and job listings are broadly applicable to any CS undergrad worldwide.

How should I read the handbook?

If you're in Year 1–2, start at Skills and Careers. If you're in Year 3 and thinking about graduate school, jump to Master's Preparation. If you're in Year 4 and actively applying, go straight to Readiness and Jobs.

How was the content organised?

We interviewed ourselves (really — we listed every question we wished we'd asked earlier), then grouped them into the seven chapters. Inside each chapter, content is ordered from "what" to "how" to "when".

Can I share it with a friend?

Please do. The site is public and the content is free to read. If you're a classmate also building a similar site, we'd love to compare notes.

How to read this handbook

If you're exploring (Year 1–2): start with Skills and Careers.

If you're planning (Year 3): focus on Master's Preparation and Careers.

If you're applying (Year 4): skip ahead to Readiness and Jobs.
